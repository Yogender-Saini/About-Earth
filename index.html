Introduction

Our atmosphere is mostly nitrogen, but there is plenty of oxygen for us to breathe. The atmosphere also protects us from incoming meteoroids, which most of the time break up in our atmosphere before hitting the ground as meteorites.

You might think that because we live here, we know everything there is to know about Earth. In fact, not at all! We still have a lot to learn about our home planet. Many satellites are currently orbiting the Earth, taking pictures and measurements. This is how we can learn more about the weather, oceans, soil, climate change, and a variety of other topics.

Structure and Surface

The planet Earth is a terrestrial planet. It's tiny and rocky.

The Earth's atmosphere is just the right thickness to keep the planet warm enough for living things like us to exist. It is the only known planet in our solar system that supports life. It is mostly nitrogen, but there is enough oxygen for us to breathe.

Time on Earth

A day on Earth is slightly less than 24 hours long.

On Earth, one year lasts 365.25 days. That 0.25 extra means we need to add one day to our calendar every four years. It's known as a leap day (in a leap year).
